About My Goals

The two goal I created are ''Sustainble Energy'' and ''Good Health and Well-Being'' They emphasize responsible resource use, environmental awareness, and physical responsibility. By implementing these goals, we can ensure a balanced approach to meeting today's needs without compromising the ability of future generations to thrive.

Concepts Learned:

  • Coordinate System: I used (x, y) positions to place each shape precisely in the canvas.
  • Basic Shapes: I created rectangles, circles, stars, and polygons to build symbolic images like solar panels and medical icons.
  • Colors and Fills: I applied colors such as gold, forestGreen, and crimson to represent different global goals and meanings.
  • Layering: I learned that shapes appear in the order they are written—new shapes go on top, which helped me design layered symbols like stars over circles.
  • Labels: I used the Label() function to add text with custom fonts, sizes, and colors, reinforcing the meaning of each side.
  • Borders and Lines: I used borders and lines to outline structures like buildings or crosses, showing how shapes can be detailed with more control.

Project Description

This project represents two United Nations Sustainable Development Goals through code-based graphics.

On the top-left side, "Sustainable Energy for All" is shown with a gold background. A white star and golden circle symbolize renewable solar energy, while geometric lines represent infrastructure for efficient energy distribution.

On the top-right side, "Good Health and Well-Being" is displayed with a forest green background. A crimson circle with a white medical cross highlights healthcare access and the universal symbol of health services.

This artwork demonstrates how coding and design can visually communicate important global goals, encouraging awareness and action toward a more sustainable future.

CMU Project: Interactive Animation

Project Description

This project illustrates a solution to ensure better healthcare access creating medical insurance plans that fit different household incomes. This promotes affordability and encourages more people to seek preventive care and visit doctors without financial stress.

Unit 2 Concepts Used:

  • Mouse Events: Used onMousePress and onMouseMove to detect user actions.
  • Animation: Used onStep to update object positions over time.
  • Conditionals: Applied if statements to control how objects react to user input.
  • Object Properties: Modified shapes (like changing color, size, or position) in real time.
  • Event-Driven Programming: The program reacts to user interaction, making it interactive and fun.
